Gabriel García Márquez

Nobel Prize in Literature

Life and work

  • 1927: Born in Aracataca, Magdalena Colombia.
  • 1948: Began his career as a journalist in some newspapers in Colombia as El Universal in Cartagena and El Espectador in Bogotá.
  • 1955: His first novel La hojarasca was published, took several years to find a publisher. Most of the edition remained in the cellar and the author did not receive a penny in royalties, Garcia Marquez said that everything he had written, La hojarasca was his favorite because they felt it was the most sincere and spontaneous.
  • 1958: He married with Mercedes Barcha, they lived abroad in the following years in Mexico City.
  • 1967: Writes the novel for which he is best known: One Hundred Years of Solitude, the maximum exposure of magic realism.
    • It was translated into 37 languages and three decades later had sold 25 million copies worldwide. The book went to bookstores without any advertising campaign, the novel sold out its first edition of 8000 copies within two weeks. He moved with his family to Barcelona, where he lived until 1975 when they left the country for fear of the decline of the Franco regime.
  • 1969: The novel (One Hundred Years of Solitude) won the Chianciano Appreciate in Italy and was named the "Best Foreign Book" in France. It was published in English and was chosen as one of the twelve best books of the year in the United States.
  • 1972: He was awarded wiht the Neustadt International Prize for Literature.
  • 1981: The liberal government of Julio Cesar Turbay Ayala, accused García Márquez of financing the guerrilla group M-19 and because for five years he had kept the Alternativa magazine, socialist-style., forcing him to flee Colombia and seek asylum in Mexico.
  • 1982: He receives the Nobel Prize in Literature.
  • 2002: García Márquez presents the first part of his memoirs, Living to Tell the Tale, in which he reviews the first thirty years of his life.
  • 2014: He died on April 17, 2014 in Mexico City after a relapse in the lymphatic cancer for which he had been treated in 1999.
